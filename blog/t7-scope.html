local, inside a function is inside scope local variables are deleted when the function that contains them is in active global variables are only deleted once the window.tab is closed .. but recreated when the page is loaded again global, outside a functions as you might of guessed is global undeclared variables that are given a value will automatically become global function scope scope determines what can see what be where its placed and how its defined variables inside a function are not seen or accessible outside of said function


Well dear friend, 'scope' as used inside JavaScript is actually quite similar to how we use it in our language and day to day lives. Of-course it has its own quirks and so now I shall elaborate. In JS there are two types of 'scope'

  • Global scope
  • Local scope
They have been quite aptly named as they explain themselves quite well. 'Global scope' is the entirety of your code (HTML/CSS included) and so any function or variable declared in the 'global scope' of your JS is visible anywhere inside your code. 'Local scope' is the code inside your 'Global functions' that have been written inside the 'Global scope'. All variables declared inside the 'local scope' ('var' scat, no-one invited you) are only visible inside the 'Local scope' 'Local' can see the 'Global' but not the other way around. A decent analogy is one of 'house rules' vs 'Country based Law', ie: It may very well be considered a kindness (or even mandatory) to hurl your food at other people inside your home. But I reckon that those house rules will not hold up so well outside of your home.
