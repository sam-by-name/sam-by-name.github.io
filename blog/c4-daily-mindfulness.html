What Chade-Meng Tan is predominantly talking about in this chapter is "Mindfulness", unfortunately ... he was not overly mindful of his readers and so it contains a large amount of filler, less pertinent to his themed message.
Meditation and Mindfulness can be used and exercised in multiple ways within and throughout our day.
But rather than regurgitate in brief what he dances in and around, I shall instead give a short variation, in the hope of reinforcing something we already knew.
Living beings of all shapes and forms, are all exquisitely unique individuals. Venturing their way through this infinitely massive, gloriously weird and wonderful Universe (whatever that infant is).
We all love feeling good, receiving positive feedback, reaching common ground and being regularly reminded how small the world really is. We all get a little lost or confused along the way, sometimes even a little scared and alone. It's so easy to feel insignificant in the ruckus of the masses.
Who am I? What am I? Why am I? How can I show and share that which makes me special? And of course what on Earth is that anyway?
Mindfulness is a word used to describe that which joins us all, the constant reminder of our inter-connectedness and through its practice we survive, strive, and thrive together.
Meditation is a word used to embody the act of reflection, not just of the self, but also of the fractalized whole. Through its practice we refresh, reconnect and rekindle our united goal.
These two acts of being occur naturally, but can be practiced to enhance life for all involved. One simple little trick to oft deploy, is to take a step back and remember Life is awesome and you are life, so ...
