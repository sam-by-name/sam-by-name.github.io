Reflect on Reflection

I got blocked on a part of the "Dom Events and Classes" assignment, After a couple attempts at making it right,
I started to look over the code to see where it was breaking, I tried rolling back to where it was working before, but no joy in finding what and where so I decided to take a break.

  • I put "Macaroni Union"'s Weightless on.
  • I sat on the floor.
  • Closed my eyes and began breathing slowly and thoughtfully.
  • After a while I started to visualize what I had just been typing.
  • Why I had been typing and the control flow of the script.

When I got back up and re-read the assignment and the code I was writing, I instantly saw that I had misread and been putting the latest code into the wrong function. A quick copy and paste and off I went.

I have been using a bit of psudocode here and there but feel like it is going to become more and more relevant as I start to write scripts from scratch.

I have not used the Rubber ducky method as yet, but I do talk though the problem with myself, i.e. voice my process, what tools I have used and what has/hasn't worked and this often works well for me.

I have done used Dev tools to locate where I am going wrong more than a few times, sometimes with success sometimes not.

I have not used much console logging as yet, but instead used Dev tools to find where the break point is.
If I cannot see the answer after using some of my tricks and methods Goggle gets a barrage of clicks and questions.

I have not been asking my peers as yet for help as yet. This is mainly due to my need to know I have thoroughly tried all my techniques and have given it my best shot before asking. But also due to wanting for those I ask to be at a similar point and as such be going through the same work as me (or have already done), which is why I have slowed my progress through through EDA's source content and over the next 1-2 weeks will be re-enforcing what I have already learnt and going through some additional material. So I can the progress in a more collaborative manner.

I find that reflection is forever imperative in growing and learning from our life lessons and experiences.
