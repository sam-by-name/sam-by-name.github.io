Tech Blog 1/2

Q. What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

A. What I see when I look at the DBC/EDA experience, is their way of teaching and growing the individual, as part of a whole and living, community.
A family outside of it's normal definition, one bound not by blood, but instead by commonly held values and goals.

Q.What are your impressions?

A.My impressions so far are that my first impressions were right on the money.
Here resides a school, a home, a place of work and growth, that I would love nothing more than to be a part of.

Q.How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

A.I can hardly wait to further immerse myself in what I have seen of the EDA culture.
To give it my all, only to be humbled and then ride the emotions that come from short team defeats.
To reflect, look deeper inside and see the still more, yet untouched gears that we all have and to learn as a group how to put them into action.

Q.Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

A.Trepidation voided, gut instincts confimed.

Q.Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

A.I am really looking forward to more EDA experience, excited to be a part of the Mamaku cohort and my nerves are as of now, resting. I have no doubt that as a whole, we can overcome inconceivable odds.
