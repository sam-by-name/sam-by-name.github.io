Your previous view on meditation, new aspects you have gleaned or that stood out?

This chapter focused heavily on the different ways one can meditate, from the old and traditional, to the new and subtle ways that we can take a moment to reflect and reconnect with ourselves. A lot of the western world's early years of teaching, revolve around getting us ready for society and how to interact within it with others. While this is in no way a bad thing to teach, sadly something quintessential to our journey through life is missed in these first steps and many go a long way without learning how to be with just themselves, how to know themselves, to understand who we the individual are and how to be ones own friend.
Meditation and its many ways of practicing it, is the nurturing of the self that we all need to live our lives to their full. It's nice to see it shown in so many simple ways, so as to dispel the overly spiritual and or religious shroud that surrounds it in so many's minds.
The main new whispers of wisdom that I walked away with this time are, that as I had previously thought, the mind is very much a muscle and that through the focusing of attention and un-focusing, intentional or otherwise it grows in strength and response time.

Any other musings?

Ah, it was nice to here about the studies regarding the effects of taped meditation instructions (received audio waves / music) being given to some of the test participants and how it had been recorded to show hastened healing. Long have I used music and sounds for a host of different circumstances, ranging from getting up and out quick like, calming, a metronome for those days that feel like a slog, syking up for the gym. It's interesting to hear of scientific proof and the releasing of it in to the wild.
