When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

When programing, one of my triggers would be when I feel like I am not progressing as fast as I know I can. This can cause frustration which certainly does not help me go any faster. Fortunately I am aware of it and catch it early on. I deal with it via a few different methods,

  • Some fresh air
  • Rapid meditation
  • Push ups
Another trigger and one that I believe gets many of us, is when I finally find the solution to something I have been racking my brain on for a while ... and find that it was actually just some silly oversight. Thats a little quirk I'v had for a long time so I actually just smile when it happens now :)


How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

The SBNRR exercise is something I have been practising (though I didn't know it's name had been coined) for quite some time. You see, I was what a fair few teachers called "naughty", I had too many questions and I responded to quickly.
So between being told to "Think before you speak" more times than I care to count (when I was a kid this is) and having an undiagnosed stomach ailment that caused me to be unable to keep food down for up to a week at a time (and subsequently lose over 20kg in one year ... no I was not a small boy :p ).
I soon found out it had been stress and because of this I learnt so much about how closely our minds well being is linked with our bodies strength and vitality. Since that realization I have spent a very decent amount of time looking inside myself, at how I react internally with regards to the external on-goings.
So to take the Siberian North Railroad is not just beneficial to what you are currently doing, it also helps with the things you are not looking at or dealing with.
